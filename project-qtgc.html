The System

A full-stack supply chain planning system that replaced gut-feel purchasing with statistical forecasting across three warehouses of a premium social club. Designed, built, and deployed solo. Currently running in production across Kitchen, General & Beverages, and Maintenance operations.

The Problem

The club operated three independent warehouses — Kitchen, General & Beverages, and Maintenance — each purchasing reactively, with no shared demand model, no cross-warehouse visibility, and no automated alerts. An ERP audit of the Kitchen warehouse alone revealed $220K+ in annual purchasing outside any control framework. The same structural problem existed across all three operations, representing a combined inventory of $248K with zero data-driven oversight.

"An ERP audit revealed 49 items with underestimated StockMaximo and 33 items with fragmented orders — LOMO FINO alone had 7.7 actual orders per month vs. 3 suggested by the model."

What Was Built

A unified supply chain planning platform deployed across three warehouses — same pipeline, same mathematical framework, parametrized per operation.

Pipeline Flow

ERP Extraction → ETL in Python → Weighted Moving Average model → Automated alert system → Google Sheets dashboard → Mobile interface for real-time stock counting.

System Architecture — ERP to Mobile Interface

What Makes It Technically Interesting

1. Production-Aware Demand Model

WMA integrates direct consumption + internal production output for Kitchen. Chicken breast demand = direct use + portioned proteins produced in-house. Before this, the model was suggesting half the required quantity for key proteins because it couldn't see the transformation layer. A reverse-frequency transformation map eliminates false insumo-product relationships from co-occurrence noise.

2. Statistical Safety Stock

95% service level using 6-month sigma with lead time adjustment per SKU — not a flat buffer percentage, but a statistically calibrated threshold per item based on its own demand volatility and reorder cycle.

3. Operational Semaphore v5

3 actionable states designed around the club's actual ordering cadence (Saturday/Sunday orders). Thresholds calibrated to 30-day coverage windows — eliminates false urgency on items the previous system flagged with 60–100 days of stock.

Operational Semaphore v5 — 3 Actionable States

4. Full CI/CD via GitHub Actions

Three trigger modes: scheduled (every Monday 7 AM), manual, and webhook from the mobile app. End-to-end run time: 60 seconds. Google Apps Script connects ERP → Physical Count → webhook in a single tap.

Scalability & Deployment

The same model was parametrized for three structurally different operational contexts — not replicated, but adapted to each warehouse's demand patterns, ordering cadence, and business logic:

  • Kitchen: Perishable proteins and fresh produce with 2–4 day shelf life. Production-aware demand model integrating transformation outputs.
  • General & Beverages: Bulk beverages and dry goods. Standard WMA with extended lead times and supplier consolidation logic.
  • Maintenance: Asset criticality-based stocking model — adapted from consumption forecasting to criticality-weighted safety stock.

Three warehouses, one codebase, zero additional infrastructure cost. Approved for expansion to Maintenance by management — validation that the system generates operational confidence at the executive level, not just technical.
