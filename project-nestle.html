Act I — The Problem

The Challenge

The infant nutrition market is highly volatile — margins shift with commodity prices, regulatory changes, and the entry of low-cost competitors from new origin countries. Management was relying on static import reports that could describe what happened last quarter, but offered no ability to model scenarios, monitor competitors in real time, or identify where premium pricing power was being left on the table.

The core question was not just analytical — it was strategic: "Where should Nestlé double down, and where is the market moving against us?"

Act II — The Engineering

Data Infrastructure

Reliable strategy requires reliable data. The starting point was a flat customs file — a single monolithic table mixing transactional records with dimensional attributes, making it slow, fragile, and impossible to extend.

  • Star Schema Architecture: Normalized the flat file into a relational model, separating dimension tables (Product, Geography, Time) from the central fact table — reducing model complexity and dramatically improving DAX query performance.
  • Advanced DAX Logic: Engineered custom measures for year-over-year competitor benchmarking, volume-weighted average pricing, and premium segment concentration indices that don't exist natively in Power BI.

Act III — The Analysis

Competitive Intelligence

With a reliable data foundation in place, the analysis focused on answering two interconnected strategic questions: who is gaining share, and at what price?

1. Segment & Pricing Matrix

This view correlates import volume against average CIF price per competitor — making it immediately visible whether Nestlé's premium pricing strategy holds up against low-cost entrants, or whether margin compression is occurring in specific segments.

2. Competitive Price Evolution

A time-series view tracking how each competitor's average import price has moved in response to market events — revealing price elasticity patterns and signaling which players are absorbing cost shocks versus passing them downstream.

Act IV — The Simulation

Financial What-If Simulator

The centerpiece of the system is an interactive financial simulator that allows management to stress-test growth assumptions in real time. By adjusting a single parameter — expected market growth rate — the model instantly projects the downstream impact on EBITDA, volume mix, and portfolio revenue concentration.

This transformed the dashboard from a descriptive tool into a decision-making instrument — enabling the team to walk into quarterly business reviews with pre-modeled scenarios rather than post-hoc explanations.

What-If Financial Simulator — EBITDA Scenario Modeling Act V — The Outcome

Strategic Impact

The platform identified a $490k revenue opportunity concentrated in two underserved premium segments where Nestlé held pricing power that competitors couldn't match on cost structure. By combining competitive intelligence with scenario simulation, management could prioritize SKU investments and defend margin in the segments most exposed to low-cost substitution — shifting from reactive to proactive market strategy.
